Trade-In FAQs

How is my trade-in value determined?

We determine your trade-in value based on several factors including your vehicle's year, make, model, mileage, condition, optional features, and current market demand. We use industry-standard valuation tools and current auction data to ensure you receive a fair market value offer.

What documents do I need for a trade-in?

You'll need your vehicle title, registration, driver's license, and any loan payoff information if you still owe money on the vehicle. If the title is held by a lienholder, we can help you navigate the payoff process.

Can I trade in a vehicle that I still owe money on?

Yes, you can trade in a vehicle that has an outstanding loan balance. We'll pay off the remaining balance to your lender directly and apply any positive equity to your new vehicle purchase. If you owe more than the vehicle's value (negative equity), we can discuss options to roll that amount into your new financing.

How does trading in save me money on taxes?

In most states, you only pay sales tax on the difference between your trade-in value and the purchase price of your new vehicle. For example, if you buy a $30,000 vehicle and receive $10,000 for your trade-in, you'll only pay sales tax on $20,000, resulting in significant savings.
